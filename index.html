Hi everyone! I’m Rohit, and I’m currently a fourth-year student at Georgia Tech majoring in computer science.

I tried to nurture these interests through both research and industry. Last summer, I worked at Instagram, engineering solutions to understand and redress the underlying factors for low ad submission rates using machine learning and data science. The next semester, I interned at Amazon Web Services—I was tasked with researching methods to migrate QuickSight dashboards across business accounts.

My interest in software engineering stems from my first internship at the Reynolds Lab at UT Southwestern, where I developed a fervent interest in computational modeling, machine learning, and data engineering: areas of computing that I still find interesting to this day. As such, my thread concentrations are in Modeling and Simulation and Artificial Intelligence.

At Georgia Tech, I conduct research for the BorgLab under Prof. Frank Dellaert as a product of my interest in robotics, computer vision, localization, and mapping. I also work for corporate relations at the College of Computing and help lead the computational biology and supercomputing clubs. Outside my professional life, I love hiking, exploring the outdoors, and spending time with my wonderful friends.

Feel free to reach out at rohitvemuri@gatech.edu if you want to chat!
